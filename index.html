A Neural Fractal

This is a neural fractal.

It's an attempt at visualising the fractal nature of training neural networks.

Every point in this picture represents the fate of a single training run.

The network we are training is this very tiny MLP:

fθ(u) = Σi vi · tanh(wi u + bi)i = 0…3

with loss function:

L(θ) = ½ Σ (fθ(u) − sin 2u)²4 points

Simply put, it fits 4 points on the curve sin(2u)

The interesting bits occur when we start experimenting with the initialization of the weights. Let's pick sine and cosine to initialize from and let's scale our picks by scalars x and y.

wi = x · sin(1.7 i + 1)
vi = y · cos(2.3 i + 0.5)i = 0…3

Finally, we train our network with 600 iterations

θ ← θ − η · ∇L× 600

Now we record one fact — does the loss settle, or blow up? We store that fact as a pixel value at the location determined by our scalars x and y.

The resulting image is the neural fractal. An emergent pattern reminiscent of a biological cell, generated entirely by its artificial cousin.

Kinda poetic.

— inspired by Sohl-Dickstein, The boundary of neural network trainability is fractal (2024)
